About

What I do.

I study how things work and work to make them useful. I received my B.S. and M.S. in Physics from UT Dallas, where I am currently pursuing a PhD. In my studies I have learned once you understand the nature of a thing, you can leverage it to solve problems.

During my time at UT Dallas I have been privileged to conduct research with Dr. David J. Lary and a diverse team of graduate, undergraduate, and high school students. The common theme throughout our research is utilizing tools from artificial intelligence, automation, and data science to promote scientific discovery and public service. Our projects span several scales and spaces, from building and calibrating low cost weather sensor networks, deploying a robotic water and aerial vehicle team, and studying environmental impacts on cognitive load and performance.

I have come to find what an exciting time it is to be involved in scientific research using AI and data science. These tools are more accessible than ever before. Opening up the door to countless use cases in practically all fields.

Outside my academic and professional projects, I am a connoisseur of various breads and bread affiliates. This is almost matched by my love of spending time with friends and family. In my downtime, I enjoy pursuing creative endeavors such as making music, writing, and painting.
